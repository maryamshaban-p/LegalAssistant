Title: Freedom of Expression
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Guarantees the right to speak, write, and publish freely, subject to legal limits.
Examples / Use Cases:

Publishing political criticism.

Online blogs and social media posts.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 19: Freedom of expression is protected.

Article 22: Limits exist for hate speech, incitement, and defamation.
Notes / Exceptions:

National security laws may restrict expression.

Title: Right to Equality
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Prohibits discrimination based on race, religion, gender, disability, or social status.
Examples / Use Cases:

Employment hiring practices.

Access to education and public services.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 3: Citizens are equal before the law.

Article 10: Discrimination in government services is prohibited.
Notes / Exceptions:

Positive discrimination policies for marginalized groups are allowed.

Title: Right to Privacy
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Protects personal data, private life, and communications.
Examples / Use Cases:

Unauthorized surveillance.

Data collection by companies or government.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 21: Collection and processing of personal data must be lawful.

Article 25: Violations carry civil and criminal penalties.
Notes / Exceptions:

Government may collect data for security purposes with court authorization.

Title: Freedom of Assembly
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Right to peaceful protest, gathering, and association.
Examples / Use Cases:

Organizing public demonstrations.

Joining civil society organizations.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 30: Peaceful assembly is permitted.

Article 32: Prior notification may be required.
Notes / Exceptions:

Public order, safety, and national security can impose limits.

Title: Right to Access Justice
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Guarantees access to courts and legal remedies.
Examples / Use Cases:

Filing lawsuits without undue delay.

Legal aid for low-income individuals.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 35: Legal assistance must be available to all.

Article 38: Courts must provide fair and timely hearings.

Title: Anti-Discrimination Laws
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Protects vulnerable groups against discrimination in all sectors.
Examples / Use Cases:

Discrimination based on disability in employment.

Gender equality in government services.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 40: Discriminatory practices are punishable.

Title: Protection of Vulnerable Groups
Category: Civil-Rights
Description: Special provisions for children, elderly, and persons with disabilities.
Examples / Use Cases:

Legal guardianship for minors.

Accessibility standards in public buildings.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 45: Rights of vulnerable groups must be safeguarded.
