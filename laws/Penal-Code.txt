Title: Criminal Offenses
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Defines crimes and punishments under Egyptian law.
Examples / Use Cases:

Theft, assault, and property crimes.

Legal age of criminal responsibility.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 302: Theft punishable by imprisonment or fines.

Article 17: Criminal responsibility starts at age 15.

Title: Cybercrime Law
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Covers illegal online activities.
Examples / Use Cases:

Hacking, phishing, identity theft.

Cyber fraud and online harassment.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 21: Unauthorized system access is a crime.

Article 25: Identity theft punishable by imprisonment.

Title: Theft and Property Crimes
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Includes burglary, robbery, embezzlement, and property damage.
Examples / Use Cases:

Stealing office equipment.

Arson or vandalism.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 310: Burglary carries imprisonment.

Article 315: Compensation required for property damage.

Title: Assault and Personal Injury
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Covers physical harm, battery, and abuse.
Examples / Use Cases:

Domestic violence.

Assault in public areas.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 320: Assault penalties depend on severity.

Article 325: Repeat offenders face increased punishment.

Title: Drug-Related Offenses
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Possession, trafficking, and abuse of controlled substances.
Examples / Use Cases:

Illegal drug distribution.

Use of narcotics without prescription.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 335: Possession of controlled substances carries imprisonment.

Article 340: Trafficking carries harsher penalties.

Title: White-Collar Crimes
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Fraud, bribery, embezzlement, and corruption.
Examples / Use Cases:

Corporate fraud or tax evasion.

Bribing public officials.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 350: Fraud penalties include fines and imprisonment.

Article 355: Bribery is punishable regardless of amount.

Title: Juvenile Justice
Category: Penal-Code
Description: Special rules for offenders under 18.
Examples / Use Cases:

Rehabilitation programs for juvenile offenders.

Court procedures for minors.
Key Articles / Rules:

Article 360: Juveniles subject to separate trials.

Article 365: Emphasis on rehabilitation over punishment.
